Neural Networks



Neural networks are machine learning models inspired by how the human brain processes information. They’re made up of layers of nodes, or “neurons,” that pass signals forward after applying simple transformations. By stacking many layers together, the network can gradually learn and represent very complex patterns in data. Training happens by adjusting the connections (or weights) between neurons so the model improves at predicting the right outcomes.

Different network architectures are built for different kinds of problems. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs), for example, are especially good at image-related tasks because they can detect patterns like edges and textures. Recurrent networks (RNNs) were once popular for sequential data like text or time series, but more recently, transformers have become the dominant architecture for natural language processing because of their ability to handle long-range relationships in text.


Deep learning, which uses large and multi-layered neural networks, has pushed machine learning far beyond traditional methods. These models now power cutting-edge applications in speech recognition, computer vision, and generative AI, making them one of the most important drivers of recent progress in artificial intelligence.
