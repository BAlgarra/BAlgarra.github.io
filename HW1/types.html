Model Types & Techniques



Machine learning relies on a variety of model types and techniques that allow algorithms to learn from data in different ways. Broadly, these can be categorized into supervised, unsupervised, semi-supervised, and reinforcement learning. In supervised learning, models are trained on labeled data to perform tasks such as classification (predicting categories) or regression (predicting continuous values). Common supervised models include linear regression, logistic regression, support vector machines (SVMs), and decision trees. Unsupervised learning, on the other hand, deals with unlabeled data, with techniques like clustering (e.g., k-means) and dimensionality reduction (e.g., principal component analysis, or PCA) used to uncover hidden patterns. Semi-supervised learning combines elements of both, while reinforcement learning trains agents through trial-and-error interactions with an environment.


Another important family of techniques is ensemble learning, which improves performance by combining multiple models. Methods such as bagging (e.g., random forests), boosting (e.g., AdaBoost, XGBoost), and stacking leverage the strengths of different models to achieve higher accuracy and robustness. Probabilistic models, including naïve Bayes classifiers and Bayesian networks, incorporate probability theory to handle uncertainty in data.

In recent years, neural networks and deep learning have become prominent, extending the scope of model types beyond traditional approaches. Neural networks consist of layers of interconnected nodes (or “neurons”) that can approximate complex functions. Architectures like convolutional neural networks (CNNs) excel in image processing, while transformers dominate natural language processing. These techniques represent a major shift in ML, enabling state-of-the-art performance across domains such as speech recognition, computer vision, and generative AI.
